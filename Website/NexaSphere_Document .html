What We Do

A curated set of activities designed to push students beyond classrooms — into real-world thinking, building, and collaboration.

01
Competition

Hackathon

We conduct inter-college and national-level hackathons where teams build real products under time pressure — and also send our best teams to represent NexaSphere at top national hackathons like Smart India Hackathon, HackWithInfy, and GFG Hackathons.

02
Competition

Codathon

We host internal and inter-college competitive coding contests on platforms like Codeforces, LeetCode, and HackerRank — and actively encourage participation in national-level contests like Code Gladiators, TCS CodeVita, and Google Code Jam.

03
Innovation

Ideathon

We run campus-level idea pitching events across themes like Social Impact, EdTech, FinTech, and Smart Campus — and also prepare teams for national-level ideathons such as NASSCOM Ideathon, Toycathon, and government innovation challenges.

04
Learning

Workshop

We conduct hands-on workshops including Git & GitHub, Web Development (HTML/CSS/JS, React), Android with Kotlin, Python & ML basics, Cloud (GCP/AWS), Docker & DevOps, Prompt Engineering, Figma & UI/UX, and Cybersecurity fundamentals.

05
Knowledge

Insight Session

We organise focused sessions on topics like How to Crack Internships, Roadmap to GSoC, Cloud Certifications Explained, AI Trends in 2025, Career in Cybersecurity, and Open Source 101 — short, sharp, and immediately actionable.

06
Community

Open Source Contribution Day

We dedicate full-day sessions to contributing to real open-source projects on GitHub — fixing issues, writing docs, and submitting PRs — and actively prepare members for programmes like GSSoC, Hacktoberfest, and Google Summer of Code.

07
Critical Thinking

Tech Debate

We host structured debates on real tech controversies — AI vs Human Jobs, iOS vs Android, Centralisation vs Web3, SQL vs NoSQL — building the ability to form, defend, and challenge technical opinions with confidence.

NexaSphere — Club Identity
01 Vision

To be the most impactful student-driven technology ecosystem in the region.

NexaSphere envisions a campus where every student — regardless of branch or background — has equal access to cutting-edge technology exposure, industry-grade learning, and a thriving community that turns curiosity into capability and ideas into impact.

02 Mission

To bridge the gap between academic learning and real-world technology.

Our mission is to create a structured, inclusive, and opportunity-rich environment that unifies all technical communities under one governance, fosters cross-domain collaboration, and ensures students graduate not just with degrees — but with demonstrable skills, open-source contributions, and the confidence to build.

03 Objectives

What we set out to achieve — every semester.

Provide students with industry-relevant technical skills beyond the standard curriculum through workshops and live project sessions.
Cultivate a strong developer and innovation culture on campus by hosting regular hands-on competitions and collaborative builds.
Unify all technical communities — Cloud, Web, AI/ML, Android, Cybersecurity — under a single, structured governance system.
Connect students with global technology ecosystems including Google Developer Groups, open-source communities, and national tech events.
Build a sustainable, student-driven leadership pipeline where members grow into mentors and organisers for future cohorts.
Ensure equal opportunity for all students — from beginners to advanced learners — through tiered learning paths and inclusive event design.
Enhance institutional reputation through Google-backed programmes, documented impact, and measurable student outcomes.
